Welcome to Georgia Al-Anon/Alateen Area 11.

Welcome to Newcomers
We want to extend a warm welcome to newcomers visiting our Georgia Area 11 World Wide WEB Site. If you are concerned about someone else’s drinking, we encourage you to browse our web site for information about our programs and Georgia meeting information.
 
 
Our program of recovery is adapted from Alcoholics Anonymous and is based upon the Twelve Steps, Twelve Traditions, and the Twelve Concepts of Service.
 
The only requirement for membership is that there be a problem of alcoholism in a relative or friend. Al-Anon/Alateen is not affiliated with any other organization or outside entity.
 
Groups
Al-Anon meetings are held in 112 countries. There are over 25,000 Al-Anon and 3,000 Alateen groups worldwide. There are over 270 meetings in Georgia.
 
Literature and Materials
Our publications are based upon the shared experience of our membership and their application of Al-Anon’s principles to their lives. Literature is available through Al-Anon Central Services of Georgia, WSO and MAIS.
